Research Portfolio

Below are papers I wrote that required research involving data collection and analysis, literature reviews, and interviews.

User Experience

  • This Yelp iPhone App Survey Report was created for my User Experience class. The purpose of conducting the survey was to determine the demographics of users and non-users of the Yelp Application (App) for the iPhone, gauge how frequently people use the Yelp App and its competitors for searching local information, to determine user satisfaction with the Yelp App and what features of the Yelp App are being used, and to discover why some iPhone users do not use the Yelp App. My group created a satisfaction survey using Google Forms. Individually, we had to write a report. I used descriptive and analytical statistics to analyze the data. The results are contained here in the report.

Proposal

  • I wrote this proposal to improve the Alfred Caldwell Lily Pool for a class on Chicago Public Spaces at DePaul University. I took an existing proposal for renovation and added additional arguments for moving forward with the improvements. Most of the images of the lily pool are my own. In addition to conducting a literature review for my paper, I also interviewed a docent who knew Alfred Caldwell personally as well as the director of the Lincoln Park Conservancy. I presented this paper at the First Year Writing Showcase and won first prize.
