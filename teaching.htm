Teaching Portfolio

Below are documents I used to facilitate instructional presentations. I have additional experience creating instructional documents on using software or guidelines to office procedures and protocol.

Customizing Course Management System

  • As a Writing Intern for DePaul University's First Year Program, I organized and redesigned their course management system and revised and authored new course materials. The Associate Director asked me to give a presentation to new student mentors on how to navigate the newly redesigned course management system and teach them how to customize their own systems. I created this PowerPoint for my presentation to over 100 new freshmen mentors. I showed them not only how to design their own systems, but also how to take advantage of the features, like surveys and discussion boards, and connect them with the larger learning goals of the DePaul University's First Year Program, such as Sense of Belonging and Community and Environmental Sustainability.

Time Management

  • As a freshmen mentor, I made it a priority to customize my lesson plans to fit my students' needs. The first year program was built around pre-written syllabi and lesson plans, which is great because starting from scratch is always a long process. However, I was always conscious to not deliver the lessons systematically. Especially around midterms, I would have students reflect on their progress as a whole. One theme that came up was issues with time management. In order to address this problem, I put together a time management activity. This activity forced students to detail how they spent their time. The goal was to have them see how they were spending their time. It was an eye-opening experience for many of my students and they were really grateful for the activity.
