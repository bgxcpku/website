Below are examples of documents I have written for academic credit. I also have experience writing professional documents, such as proposals, executive status reports, and operational instructions.

Senior Honors Thesis

  • The attribution of Singapore’s unrivaled economic growth to its authoritarian government has come to be known as the “Lee Thesis” named after the first Prime Minister of Singapore, Lee Kuan Yew. Combining my two interests together, Singapore and the role of the government in a multicultural society, I wrote my Honors Thesis to address my research question to be “Is the Lee Thesis right as defended by Lee or flawed as argued by Sen? How is it that Singaporeans are happy despite their lack of freedoms?”. Defenders and supporters of the Lee Thesis argue their position by oversimplifying Singapore’s history and context. Singapore’s complex reality makes it difficult to defend the totality of either side.

Instruction Manual

  • I led a team project for creating an instructional guide on how to spend less money as a DePaul student. I held many responsibilities, which included: delegating assignments to research and write, designing the manual, editing initial drafts, and providing the instructor with progress reports in the form of a memorandum. I also designed a usability test and wrote the usability report. This experience amplified my technical communication interest because it allowed me to clearly connect theory with practice. Although there are standard aspects of the process of document creation such as editing, drafting, and collaborating with others, there is room for creativity as it relates to the document’s purpose. It is this awareness that makes technical communication fascinating and fun.
