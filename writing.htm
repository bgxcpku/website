  • Thesis
  • After reading Amartya Sen's "Development as Freedom", I had been fascinated with the country, Singapore. Growing up in a democratic country, it seemed odd how a country, with an arguably authoritarian country, could have one of the highest happiness ratings in the world. This thesis investigates the idea of the "good life" in Singapore and compares it to differing ideas in other countries and cultures. This thesis takes an interdisciplinary approach by drawing from theories in political science, economics, philosophy, and psychology.
