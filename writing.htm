  • The attribution of Singapore’s unrivaled economic growth to its authoritarian government has come to be known as the “Lee Thesis” named after the first Prime Minister of Singapore, Lee Kuan Yew. Combining my two interests together, Singapore and the role of the government in a multicultural society, I wrote my Honors Thesis to address my research question to be “Is the Lee Thesis right as defended by Lee or flawed as argued by Sen? How is it that Singaporeans are happy despite their lack of freedoms?”. Defenders and supporters of the Lee Thesis argue their position by oversimplifying Singapore’s history and context. Singapore’s complex reality makes it difficult to defend the totality of either side.
